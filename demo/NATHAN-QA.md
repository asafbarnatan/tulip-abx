# Nathan Q&A

Glance, deliver, move on. No hedging, no apologizing.

**Q1. Is the campaign connected via integration?**
*"Not yet — manually for now. The wiring is there for LinkedIn, ZoomInfo, and Salesforce; once Tulip gives me the credentials, they all flip on. Even then, I'd want a human to hit publish."*

**Q2. What was the hardest part to build?**
*"Early on, the agents filled gaps that weren't grounded — invented customer names, stats. I had to keep tightening the guardrails. Zero-fabrication is now baked into every prompt."*

**Q3. Could you start at Tulip tomorrow?**
*"Yes — the platform is ready to deploy. First step is per-user access so every action is traceable to a specific AE, then plug in LinkedIn, ZoomInfo, and Salesforce. Real day-one."*

**Q4. If you worked at Tulip, what would you want first?**
*"Interviews with the real users — AEs, sales, marketing, CS. Today the platform runs on my assumptions; with their input it becomes real decision support."*

**Q5. Who is the target audience for the Bayer campaign?**
*"Bayer employees in Germany only, English-speaking, in 17 specific senior roles — VP Quality Assurance, Director of Manufacturing IT, Head of GxP Compliance, Site Director, Director of Validation, Head of Process Engineering, and a handful of related Quality and Mfg-IT decision-makers. The people who actually own the paper batch-record problem we're solving."*

**Q6. How long did it take you to build this?**
*"About 4-5 days. First half was setup and the agent pipeline; back half was content corrections, design polish, and demo readiness."*

**Q7. Show me where an agent broke. What did you do?**
*"Early on, one of the agents made up a customer name. I tightened the rules so each agent only uses real, verifiable information — and because every agent builds on the output of the one before it, the work gets sharper as the pipeline runs. Anything they still miss, the team can edit inline."*

**Q8. Show me a moment where the human matters more than the agent.**
*"Publish to LinkedIn. Every campaign saves as a draft; an AE reviews and clicks publish. Same for plays and brief approval — agents draft, humans decide."*

**Q9. If I rolled this out at Tulip Monday, how long until an AE sees value?**
*"First Mission Control open. They see prioritized accounts, a generated brief per account, three drafted plays with openers. Cold-start eliminated. Whether they ship is their call."*

**When you don't know:** *"Honest answer — I haven't built that yet. Here's how I'd think about it…"* — then move on.
