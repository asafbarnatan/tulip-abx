# Nathan Q&A

Glance, deliver, move on. No hedging, no apologizing.

**Q1. Is the campaign connected via integration?**
*"No — manual today. Same story for LinkedIn, ZoomInfo, and Salesforce: infrastructure is wired, OAuth scaffolded; the moment Tulip provides credentials, all three flip on live. Even then, I'd keep a human in the loop on publish."*

**Q2. What was the hardest part to build?**
*"Early on, the agents filled gaps that weren't grounded — invented customer names, stats. I had to keep tightening the guardrails. Zero-fabrication is now baked into every prompt."*

**Q3. Could you start at Tulip tomorrow?**
*"Yes — first I'd set up per-user access so every action is traceable to a specific AE, then plug in LinkedIn, ZoomInfo, and Salesforce. Credible week-one."*

**Q4. If you worked at Tulip, what would you want first?**
*"Interviews with the real users — AEs, sales, marketing, CS. Today the platform runs on my assumptions; with their input it becomes real decision support."*

**Q5. Who is the target audience for the Bayer campaign?**
*"Bayer employees in Germany only, English locale, 17 specific VP+ job titles in Quality and Manufacturing IT (the people who own paper batch records). LinkedIn ad set ID 690308904, reach ≈37 unique members over 4 days, 46 impressions, 4 engagements, $44 spent. Narrow on purpose — that's what an ABM campaign should look like."*

**Q6. How long did it take you to build this?**
*"~10 days end-to-end with Claude Code. First half was setup and the agent pipeline; back half was content corrections, design polish, and demo readiness."*

**Q7. Why is this different from ChatGPT or 6sense?**
*"ChatGPT doesn't know who Tulip's customers are or which framings Nathan rejects. 6sense ranks accounts but doesn't write the brief or the ad. This stitches intel, positioning, plays, and campaigns into one editable workflow grounded in Tulip-specific rules."*

**Q8. Show me where an agent broke. What did you do?**
*"Early Bayer brief had a fabricated 'DePuy Synthes is a Tulip customer' claim. I added a verified-roster file, made every prompt cite or stay silent, and the editable-brief flow catches what the agents still miss."*

**Q9. Show me a moment where the human matters more than the agent.**
*"Publish to LinkedIn. Every campaign saves as a draft; an AE reviews and clicks publish. Same for plays and brief approval — agents draft, humans decide."*

**Q10. If I rolled this out at Tulip Monday, how long until an AE sees value?**
*"First Mission Control open. They see prioritized accounts, a generated brief per account, three drafted plays with openers. Cold-start eliminated. Whether they ship is their call."*

**When you don't know:** *"Honest answer — I haven't built that yet. Here's how I'd think about it…"* — then move on.
