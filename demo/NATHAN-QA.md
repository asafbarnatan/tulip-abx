# Nathan Q&A — interview cheatsheet

Anticipated questions from Nathan. One-sentence answers in your voice. Glance, deliver, move on. No hedging, no apologizing.

### Q1. Is the LinkedIn campaign connected through an integration?
> *"No — manual today. Infrastructure is built. The moment Tulip's Marketing API is available, it flips on live. Even then, I'd keep a human in the loop on publish."*

### Q2. What was the hardest part to build?
> *"Early on, the agents filled in gaps that weren't grounded — they'd invent customer names or stats. I had to keep tightening the guardrails. That zero-fabrication contract is now baked into every agent prompt."*

### Q3. Could you start working on this at Tulip tomorrow?
> *"Yes. I'd stand up multi-user access so each AE works under their own account, then plug in LinkedIn, ZoomInfo, and Salesforce. That's a credible week-one."*

### Q4. If you worked at Tulip, what would you want first?
> *"Interviews with the real users — AEs, sales, marketing, CS. Today the platform runs on my assumptions. With their input, it becomes real decision support, not a demo."*

## When you don't know
> *"Honest answer — I haven't built that yet. Here's how I'd think about it…"* — that's the answer. Move on.
